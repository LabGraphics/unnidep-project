MAKING A DIFFERENCE AND BRINGING HOPE ONE ISSUE AT A TIME.

You are here because you are either a member or are looking to learn more about UNNIDEP. We say thank you for your interest in Our Organization. UNNIDEP was establised in 2016 solely as a NONPOLITICAL, NOT FOR PROFIT Organization committed to making a difference in lives of the citizen of Nimba County and Liberia at large. The need to help the women, children, and men of Nimba County is dire and help could not come soon enough. The challenges of our people are enormous, these challenges are a matter life and death. Our people's access to basic food, education, and healthcare is far in between.

DONATE JOIN OUR CAUSE CURRENT PROJECTS

One does not necessarily have to be a millionare, a celebrity, or an athlete to make an impact in the lives of those that are poor and desolate. All you need is the heart and the will to want to make a difference one individual or issue at a time.

Mother Theresa once said " Not all of us can do great things but we can do small things with great love". Those small things could be providing school supplies to a child that is desperately needing to learn but lacks the bare necessities. Or renovating a dilapidated or makeshift school house that would give students an atmosphere conducive to learing.

We can not overstress the many challenges the men, women, and children of Nimba County are faced with, but also we just can not under stated the need for us to lend a hand. Making a difference and improving lives one issue or individual at a time is our committment to the citizens of Nimba County and Liberia at large. Join us in our cause, become a member/partner or donate.

HEALTHCARE EDUCATION

HEALTHCARE:
Liberia today is emerging from a brutal civil war that reducded the country's infrastructures and institutions to nothing. Although, the war has since ended, its lingering effects continue to take its toll on many liberians living in Liberia today. Today, many liberians lack access to basic healthcare services which translates into high infant mortality rates, increased mental in illneses, childhood malnutrition, and deaths.
